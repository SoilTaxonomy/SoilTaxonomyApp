The salic horizon is a saline horizon in which salts more soluble than gypsum have accumulated.
A salic horizon is must be 15 cm or more thick and has, for 90 consecutive days or more in normal years:
1 - An electrical conductivity (EC) equal to or greater than 30 dS/m in the water extracted from a saturated paste; and
2 - A product of the EC, in dS/m, and thickness, in cm, equal to 900 or more.
