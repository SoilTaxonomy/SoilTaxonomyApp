A more or less minimally developed surface horizon, typically thin or light colored, that does not meet the criteria for any other epipedon.
The ochric epipedon fails to meet the definitions for any of the other seven epipedons because it is too thin or too dry, has too high a color value or chroma, contains too little organic carbon, has too high n value or melanic index, or is both massive and hard or harder when dry.
