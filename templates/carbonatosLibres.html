Soil carbonates that are uncoated or unbound and that effervesce visibly or audibly when treated with cold, dilute HCl.
The term “free carbonates” is nearly synonymous with the term “calcareous.” Soils that have free carbonates generally have calcium carbonate as a common mineral, although sodium and magnesium carbonates are also included in this concept.
There is no implication of pedogenesis in the concept of free carbonates, as there is in identifiable secondary carbonates.

A drop of dilute HCl applied to a soil sample is widely used as a quick test to confirm the presence of carbonate
