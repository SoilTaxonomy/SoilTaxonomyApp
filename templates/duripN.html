The duripan is a root-restrictive subsoil layer that is primarily cemented by illuvial silica. Other cementing agents, such as calcium carbonate, may also be present.

Generalized Characteristics
     1) More than 50% of the layer is cemented with illuvial silica.
     2) The slake test (air dry in 1N HCl) results in < 50% dissolution of cemented fragments.
