Soils developed in environments under prolonged water saturation and seasonal alternational between water logging and drainage.
Their morphology is chacterized by the pressence of redoximorphyc features.
These water saturation cycles affect the O2 suply and consecuently the oxidation state of iron, manganese and sulfur that strongly influences their solubility and colour, explaining the brown, gray, blue, black and yellow mottles typical in hydromorphyc soils.
